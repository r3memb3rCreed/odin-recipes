Spaghetti: Pasta Salad Recipe(California Style)

Description:

Spaghetti is a classic and well-loved pasta dish that originates from Italian cuisine. It consists of long, thin cylindrical noodles made from wheat flour and water. The pasta is typically cooked until it reaches an al dente texture, which means it is cooked through but still retains a slight firmness when bitten into.
To prepare spaghetti, a large pot of salted water is brought to a rolling boil. The dried spaghetti noodles are added to the boiling water and stirred occasionally to prevent them from sticking together. The cooking time can vary depending on the desired doneness, but it usually takes around 8 to 10 minutes.
Once the spaghetti is cooked, it is drained in a colander to remove the excess water. It is then typically served with a variety of sauces and toppings. One popular choice is a tomato-based sauce, such as marinara or bolognese. The sauce is made by simmering tomatoes, herbs, garlic, and sometimes meat for a rich and flavorful base. The cooked spaghetti is mixed with the sauce, ensuring that each strand of pasta is coated evenly.
In addition to tomato-based sauces, other options include creamy Alfredo sauce, olive oil and garlic, or pesto sauce made from fresh basil, pine nuts, garlic, Parmesan cheese, and olive oil. These sauces can be customized with additional ingredients like sautéed vegetables, mushrooms, or cooked meats like chicken or shrimp.
Once the spaghetti is tossed with the sauce, it is often garnished with grated Parmesan or Romano cheese, fresh herbs like basil or parsley, and a sprinkle of black pepper or chili flakes for added flavor and visual appeal.
Spaghetti is typically served as a main course and enjoyed with a fork twirling the pasta strands onto a spoon for easier eating. It is a versatile dish that can be adapted to suit different tastes and dietary preferences, making it a favorite choice for family dinners, casual meals, and Italian-themed gatherings.

Ingredients -

Dressing:

  • 2 tablespoons white wine vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice
  • 2 tablespoons freshly grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 ½ teaspoons Italian seasoning
  • 1 ½ teaspoons honey
  • 1 clove minced garlic
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • ¼ teaspoon salt
  • ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • ⅓ cup olive oil

Salad:

  • 1 pound thin spaghetti
  • 1 cup baby spinach, rinsed and dried
  • 1 cup chopped broccoli
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1 carrot, grated
  • ½ red onion, thinly sliced
  • ½ cucumber, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 Roma tomato, diced
  • 4 ounces crumbled goat cheese, or to taste
  • ¼ cup toasted sliced almonds, or to taste
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Directions:

Steps -

  1. Whisk together white wine vinegar, lemon juice, Parmesan cheese, Italian seasoning, honey, garlic, Dijon mustard, basil, salt, and pepper in a small bowl until well combined. Gradually mix in olive oil, whisking constantly. Set dressing aside.
  2. Bring a large pot of lightly salted water to a boil. Cook spaghetti in the boiling water, stirring occasionally, until tender yet firm to the bite, about 12 minutes.
  3. While spaghetti cook, prepare all the vegetables. Combine spinach, broccoli, peas, carrot, red onion, cucumber, and tomato in a large bowl.
  4. Drain pasta, and add to the bowl with the vegetables. Toss pasta and veggies together. Pour dressing over pasta and veggies and toss until thoroughly combined. Add in goat cheese and almonds and toss again to combine.
  5. Cover pasta, and chill in the fridge for at least 3 hours to overnight. Taste pasta salad, and season with more salt and pepper, if necessary.
