Fried Rice: Recipe

Description:

Fried rice is a flavorful and versatile dish that is commonly found in various Asian cuisines. It is made by stir-frying cooked rice with a combination of vegetables, proteins, seasonings, and sometimes sauces. The result is a delicious and satisfying one-pot meal.
To prepare fried rice, leftover cooked rice is often used because it has a drier texture, which helps to prevent the dish from becoming too mushy. The rice grains should be separated and cooled before cooking to ensure that they do not clump together during the stir-frying process.
In a hot wok or large frying pan, oil is heated, and diced onions, garlic, and other aromatics are sautéed until fragrant. Vegetables such as carrots, peas, bell peppers, and corn are commonly added and stir-fried until they are cooked but still retain a slight crunch.
Proteins like diced chicken, shrimp, beef, or tofu can be included to add extra flavor and texture. These proteins are usually cooked separately and then added to the pan along with the vegetables.
Once the vegetables and proteins are cooked to the desired tenderness, the cooked rice is added to the pan. The rice is stirred and tossed with the other ingredients, ensuring that it is coated with the flavors from the aromatics, vegetables, and proteins. At this point, soy sauce, oyster sauce, or other seasonings can be added to enhance the taste and give the fried rice its signature savory flavor. Some variations may also include scrambled eggs, which are cooked separately and then mixed into the rice.
The ingredients are continuously tossed and stirred over high heat, allowing the rice to heat through and develop a slightly crispy texture on the outside. This process also helps to distribute the flavors evenly throughout the dish.
Once the fried rice is cooked, it is typically garnished with chopped green onions, cilantro, or sesame seeds for added freshness and visual appeal. It can be served as a main course or as a side dish alongside other Asian-inspired dishes.
Fried rice is a popular and convenient choice for a quick and delicious meal. It offers endless possibilities for customization, allowing individuals to add their favorite ingredients and adapt the flavors to suit their preferences. Whether enjoyed on its own or paired with other dishes, fried rice is a versatile and comforting option that is loved by many.

Ingredients -

  • ⅔ cup chopped baby carrots
  • ½ cup frozen green peas
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced, or to taste (Optional)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 3 cups leftover cooked white rice
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce, or more to taste
  • 2 teaspoons sesame oil, or to taste

Directions:

Steps -

  1. Assemble Ingredients
  2. Place carrots in a small saucepan and cover with water. Bring to a low boil and cook for 3 to 5 minutes. Stir in peas, then immediately drain in a colander.
  3. Heat a wok over high heat. Pour in vegetable oil, then stir in carrots, peas, and garlic; cook for about 30 seconds. Add eggs; stir quickly to scramble eggs with vegetables.
  4. Stir in cooked rice. Add soy sauce and toss rice to coat. Drizzle with sesame oil and toss again.
  5. Serve hot and enjoy!
